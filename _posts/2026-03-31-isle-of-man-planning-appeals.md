When a planning application is refused, applicants have the right to appeal. On the Isle of Man, 3,807 planning appeals have been decided since records began — and 33.4% of them succeeded. This article analyses appeal outcomes using data from [PlanningPortal.im](https://planningportal.im).

## Appeal Statistics at a Glance

| Metric | Figure |
|--------|--------|
| Total appeals decided | 3,807 |
| Appeals allowed (won) | 999 |
| Appeals dismissed (lost) | 1,894 |
| Success rate | 33.4% |

One in three planning appeals on the Isle of Man succeeds — a meaningful figure that suggests appeal is a viable route when an application is refused.

## When Is an Appeal Worth It?

The data suggests appeals are most likely to succeed when:

- **The refusal reason is marginal** — if an officer refused on policy grounds that are open to interpretation, an independent inspector may reach a different view
- **Comparable applications have been approved** — evidence that similar developments have been permitted nearby strengthens an appeal significantly
- **The applicant can address the reason for refusal** — sometimes additional information or amended plans resolve the concern without requiring a full appeal

Appeals are less likely to succeed when refusal is on clear policy grounds, or when the proposal is significantly out of character with the area.

## The Appeal Process on the Isle of Man

Planning appeals on the Isle of Man are heard by independent inspectors appointed by the Department of Environment, Food and Agriculture (DEFA). The process typically takes several months.

Applicants can represent themselves or engage a planning consultant or solicitor for the appeal hearing.
